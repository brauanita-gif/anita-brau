# AI Evaluator Assessment Rubric

A structured scoring rubric designed to assess the competencies of AI response evaluators.

---

## Criterion 1: Bias Identification and Management

**Definition:** The candidate names at least 2 biases, describes how each could have altered their judgment, and identifies at least one countermeasure adopted.

| Score | Label | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 4 | Exemplary | Names ≥2 biases, explains impact on judgment, provides ≥1 countermeasure with a concrete example |
| 3 | Advanced | Names ≥2 biases, explains impact, but countermeasure is inadequate or absent |
| 2 | Beginner | Names only 1 bias or does not explain its impact on the evaluation |
| 1 | Inadequate | Does not name specific biases, vague or absent response |

---

## Criterion 2: Guideline Adherence

**Definition:** The candidate cites the most relevant criterion for the response evaluated, justifies the score based on received instructions rather than personal opinion.

| Score | Label | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 4 | Exemplary | Cites the most relevant criterion, justifies the score with precise reference to instructions, with a concrete example |
| 3 | Advanced | Cites the criterion and assigns the score, but justification is generic or based on personal opinion |
| 2 | Beginner | Cites the criterion but does not justify the score based on guidelines |
| 1 | Inadequate | Does not cite criteria, evaluates by intuition or response is absent |

---

## Criterion 3: Written Feedback Quality

**Definition:** The candidate uses clear language, references specific parts of the evaluated text with timestamps, indicating for each the issue found and a possible solution.

| Score | Label | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 4 | Exemplary | Clear language, cites text with timestamps, identifies the issue and proposes a solution for each |
| 3 | Advanced | Clear language, cites text with timestamps, identifies the issue but does not provide an adequate solution |
| 2 | Beginner | Clear language, cites text with timestamps, but does not indicate the issue or a solution |
| 1 | Inadequate | Vague feedback, no references to the text, or absent |

---

## Criterion 4: Score Calibration

**Definition:** The candidate provides consistent scores for similar responses and, when scores differ, justifies the difference with specific references to the text.

| Score | Label | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 4 | Exemplary | Consistent scores for similar responses; differences justified with specific text references |
| 3 | Advanced | Consistent scores for similar responses; differences justified with vague text references |
| 2 | Beginner | Consistent scores for similar responses; differences justified but without text references |
| 1 | Inadequate | Inconsistent scores with no justification |

---

## Criterion 5: Metacognition

**Definition:** The candidate confirms having followed the guidelines, acknowledges that their personal experience may have influenced the evaluation, and identifies the specific bias that may have played a role.

| Score | Label | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 4 | Exemplary | Confirms guidelines followed, acknowledges personal influence, identifies the specific bias clearly |
| 3 | Advanced | Confirms guidelines followed, acknowledges personal influence, but identifies the bias vaguely |
| 2 | Beginner | Confirms guidelines followed, acknowledges personal influence, but does not identify what may have influenced it |
| 1 | Inadequate | No reflection on own process, no self-awareness |

---

## Scoring Scale

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 4 | Exemplary | Expert behaviour, complete and with concrete examples |
| 3 | Advanced | Competent behaviour, one key element missing |
| 2 | Beginner | Partial behaviour, fundamental elements missing |
| 1 | Inadequate | Vague, absent or off-topic response |
